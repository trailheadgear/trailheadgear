📖 Sleeping Bag Buying Guide: Down vs Synthetic

Down — Light, Durable, Expensive

Down is the gold standard. Its key advantage: warmth-to-weight ratio. For the same warmth, down is 30-50% lighter than synthetic. It packs much smaller — a 20°F down bag compresses to basketball size.

Fill Power (FP): 550-600FP (entry), 700-800FP (mid-high, sweet spot for backpackers), 850-950FP (top tier). The fatal flaw: down loses ALL insulating power when wet.

Synthetic — Affordable, Wet-Proof, Heavier

Synthetic's killer advantage: it keeps you warm even when wet. Cheaper, easier to wash, tougher. Downsides: 30-50% heavier, larger packed size, degrades after 5-8 years.

🌡️ Understanding Temperature Ratings

The number on the bag is the EN/ISO lower limit, NOT comfort. Comfort is 10-15°F warmer than the listed rating. The golden rule: expected low + 15°F = the bag rating to buy.

❓ Sleeping Bag FAQ

Q: Mummy vs rectangular?

Mummy: tapered, warmest, lightest — 90% of backpackers. Rectangular: roomy, heavier, colder — best for car camping.

Q: How to wash?

1-2x per year. Use down-specific detergent (Nikwax). Never regular detergent. Dry low heat with tennis balls.

Q: How long does it last?

Down: 10-20 years with care. Synthetic: 5-8 years. Never store compressed — hang or use a breathable sack.
