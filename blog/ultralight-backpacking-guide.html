⚡ Ultralight Backpacking: Get Your Base Weight Under 10 lbs

Updated June 2025 | 10 min read

Every pound saved = miles gained on the trail.

Ultralight is not about suffering. It is about going farther with less effort. The key metric is Base Weight — all your gear minus consumables (food, water, fuel). Target: <10 lbs (4.5 kg).

The Big Three: 60% of Your Weight

Tent → Target <3 lbs

  • Best ultralight: Big Agnes Copper Spur HV UL2 — 2.5 lbs, $530
  • Best value: Tarptent Double Rainbow — 2.6 lbs, $350
  • Dirt cheap: Tarp + bug net instead of a tent, <1 lb, $50

Sleeping Bag → Target <2 lbs

  • Top tier: Feathered Friends Hummingbird UL 20 — 1 lb 7 oz, $549
  • Recommended: Western Mountaineering UltraLite — 1 lb 13 oz, $509
  • Alternative: Quilt instead of bag — Enlightened Equipment Revelation 20°, 1 lb, $335

Backpack → Target <2 lbs

  • Best: Hyperlite 2400 Southwest — 1 lb 15 oz, $379
  • Entry: Granite Gear Crown3 60 — 2 lbs 4 oz, $220

The Three "Do Not" Rules

  1. Do not pack "just in case" items — If you are 95% sure you will not use it, leave it. Emergencies can be handled with what you DO have.
  2. Every item must serve two purposes — Trekking poles = tent poles. Buff = towel + hat + neck gaiter.
  3. Weigh everything on a gram scale — Use LighterPack.com. You will be shocked what things actually weigh.

5 Overlooked Ways to Save Weight

  • Ditch stuff sacks — Sleeping bag goes straight into pack liner. Tent without its bag. Saves 1-2 oz each.
  • Trim excess webbing — Backpack straps are always too long. Cut them. Save several ounces for free.
  • Titanium pot — Toaks 750ml, 2.6 oz/$35 vs stainless 8 oz/$15. $20 difference saves 5.4 oz.
  • Buff instead of towel — Camp towel 4 oz, Buff 1 oz. Same function.
  • Smartwater bottles — 1.3 oz each vs Nalgene 6.2 oz. Use two Smartwater bottles, save 10 oz for $2.
