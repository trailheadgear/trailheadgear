💸 10 Camping Gear Mistakes That Waste Your Money

Updated June 2025 | 8 min read | Save $500+

You do not need all of this. Really.

The camping gear industry is worth billions. At least a third of it is money people did not need to spend. These are 10 mistakes even experienced campers make.

💰 How much will you save? Avoid just 3 of these mistakes and you keep at least $300-500 in your pocket.

❌ Mistake 1: Buying a 4-Season Tent Because "It Is Tougher"

Money wasted: $300+

4-season tents are heavier, pricier, and stifling in summer. They are for blizzards, not "being extra sturdy." A good 3-season tent handles 90% of weather. See our tent picks →

❌ Mistake 2: Buying a 0°F Bag for Summer Camping

Money wasted: $100-200

A 0°F bag in 50°F weather = sweating, unzipping, and paying double for warmth you never use. Pick the right rating →

❌ Mistake 3: Expensive Bag, Cheap Pad

Money wasted: $100-150 (spent wrong)

The classic: $300 sleeping bag + $30 foam pad = still cold. The ground steals 50% of your warmth. A good $150 pad + mid-range bag beats a premium bag with a garbage pad. Pad recommendations →

❌ Mistake 4: The 10-Piece Cookset

Money wasted: $30-60

You will use the pot and the spatula. The other 8 pieces collect dust. Buy 1 pot + 1 spork + 1 cup. Cookware guide →

❌ Mistake 5: Buying Yeti When RTIC Does the Same Thing

Money wasted: $100-150

Our 72-hour ice test: Yeti Tundra 45 and RTIC 52 performed nearly identically. Yeti costs 40% more. You are paying for the bear sticker. Cooler comparison →

❌ Mistake 6: Cotton Socks on the Trail

Money wasted: $0 (but the blisters cost you)

Cotton absorbs sweat, stays wet, and rubs. Blisters. A $20 pair of Merino wool socks fixes this forever.

❌ Mistake 7: 70L Pack for a Weekend

Money wasted: $100+

A big pack invites you to fill it. Weekend trip = 40-55L. Extra space gets filled with things you will carry and never use. Backpack picks →

❌ Mistake 8: Your Phone as a Flashlight

Money wasted: $0 (until you drop it — then $500+)

Camp requires two hands. Holding your phone as a flashlight = one hand = dropped phone. A $40 headlamp lasts 5 years. Headlamp picks →

❌ Mistake 9: First Pitch at the Campsite

Cost: One ruined evening

It is dark. You are reading instructions by phone light. Missing a stake. The pole went through the wrong sleeve. It took 50 minutes. Pitch it once at home first. Beginner's guide →

❌ Mistake 10: Buying Everything at Once

Money wasted: $200-500

Borrow first, buy later. Rent or borrow a tent and bag for your first trip. After 3 trips you will know exactly what matters. Buying everything upfront = 3-4 items you never touch again. Start with $200 →
