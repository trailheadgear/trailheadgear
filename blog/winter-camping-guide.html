❄️ Winter Camping Guide: Stay Warm at -20°F

Updated June 2025 | 9 min read

⚠️ Safety note: Winter camping carries real risks — hypothermia, frostbite, carbon monoxide poisoning. If you are new to camping, start in spring or fall. Test your winter setup in the backyard first.

Winter camping is magical — when you are prepared for the cold.

The 3 Challenges of Winter Camping

1. Staying warm — The ground continuously pulls heat from your body.
2. Moisture management — Breath and sweat freeze inside your gear, degrading insulation.
3. Fuel — Canister stoves barely work below freezing. Liquid fuel is your friend.

🛏️ The Sleep System: Everything

Winter sleep = good pad + good bag + dry.

Sleeping Pad: R-value 5 Minimum

R-value is thermal resistance. Summer = R-2. Winter = R≥5. How to get there:

  • Layer pads (recommended): Foam (R=2) + Inflatable (R=4.5) = R≈6.5. Cheaper than a single winter pad.
  • Example: Therm-a-Rest Z Lite (R=2) + NeoAir XLite (R=4.5) = R=6.5. Cost: $45+$210=$255.
  • Or: Therm-a-Rest NeoAir XTherm (R=7.3, $240) — one-pad solution.

Sleeping Bag: -10°F Minimum Rating

Bag ratings are survival temps, not comfort. Add 15-20°F for comfort. Buy a bag rated 20°F below your expected low. Layer a summer bag over a winter bag for +10-15°F of warmth.

⛺ 4-Season Tent: Necessary?

Not always. Below the snow line with no major storms? 3-season + warmer sleep system works. You need a 4-season tent when: winds >40 mph, snow >6 inches, or alpine/exposed terrain. Hilleberg Nallo 2 ($795) or MSR Access 2 ($700).

🔥 Winter Stoves: Liquid Fuel Wins

Canister stoves barely work at -20°C. Get MSR WhisperLite Universal ($170) — runs on canisters, white gas, or kerosene. If you must use canisters: sleep with the canister in your bag to keep it warm.

10 Winter Survival Tips

  1. Change into dry clothes before bed — daytime sweat freezes at night.
  2. Hot water bottle in sleeping bag — boil water, fill a Nalgene, put it in your bag. Warm all night + drinkable in the morning.
  3. Eat before bed — digestion generates heat. High-fat snacks.
  4. Pee bottle — leaving your bag at night costs massive heat. Wide-mouth bottle. Use it.
  5. Ventilate! — Open tent vents. Sealed = condensation = ice = wet = cold.
  6. Do not bury your face in the bag — exhaled moisture soaks the insulation.
  7. Boots stay inside the tent — outside they freeze solid by morning.
  8. Electronics in your sleeping bag — cold kills batteries. Phone and power bank sleep with you.
  9. Bring 50% more fuel — winter boil times double.
  10. Know when to retreat — uncontrollable shivering, slurred speech, numb hands/feet = get out now. The mountain will be there next time.
