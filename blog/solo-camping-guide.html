🧘 Solo Camping: The Complete Guide to Camping Alone

Updated June 2025 | 8 min read

Solo camping is the best conversation you will ever have with yourself. No compromises. No schedule. You go where you want, eat when you want, sleep when you want. But everything falls on you alone.

Solo Camping Rule #1: Safety

  • Tell someone your itinerary — Where, when you leave, when you return, campground location. Write it down. Send it.
  • Download offline maps — Maps.me or AllTrails. No signal needed.
  • Satellite messenger — Garmin inReach Mini 2 ($400). SOS + texting from anywhere. The single most important solo investment.
  • Trust your gut — If the campsite feels wrong, leave. Your intuition is rarely wrong.
  • Never tell strangers you are alone — "My friend is out hiking, I am waiting at camp."

Solo Gear: Light Is Everything

Nobody is helping you carry this. Every ounce counts:

  • Tent: 1P or 2P? — Get a 2P. 1 extra pound for space to sit inside when it rains is worth it.
  • Sleeping bag — Western Mountaineering UltraLite, 1 lb 13 oz.
  • Sleeping pad — NeoAir XLite, R=4.5, 12.5 oz.
  • Stove — MSR PocketRocket 2, 2.6 oz. Perfect for one.
  • Headlamp — Mandatory. You only have two hands.
  • Multi-tool/knife — You handle everything yourself now.

Unique Solo Gear

  • Book or Kindle — After dark, reading is the best solo activity.
  • Bluetooth speaker (low volume) — Breaks the silence, deters wildlife.
  • Notebook — Solo camping produces surprisingly deep thinking. Write it down.
  • 20,000mAh power bank — Nobody has a spare charger for you.

How to Handle Fear and Loneliness

Being scared your first solo night is normal. Here is what works:

  • Start small — Backyard first. Then a campground 5 minutes from the parking lot.
  • Earplugs — Forest sounds get amplified at night. 90% of "weird noises" are squirrels and branches.
  • Stay busy — Collect firewood, cook, take photos, write. Hands occupied = mind occupied.
  • Accept the insomnia — You might not sleep well night one. That is fine. You can nap tomorrow.
